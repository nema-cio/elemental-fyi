The architecture beneath

The Elementals are the visible surface of something more formal underneath. What follows is the architecture — the operators, the formal grammar, the encoding that lets the framework cross substrates. This is the technical room.

The language gets denser here. The page assumes you have already met the Elementals; the soft-mode work is upstairs. Where definitions are needed, they appear. Where they don't, the architecture's own working documents carry them.

What follows is provisional. It is load-bearing enough to build on and revisable enough to question. That is the framework's stance toward its own architecture, and it is the stance the reader is invited to take.

§ I — Operators

The six operators

Six operators run the diagnostic. They are the formal cousins of the six ways of noticing. Each names an operation — a thing the substrate is doing — rather than a category of content. They are written with their canonical glyphs and Greek letters.

Glyph Operator Operation
∴ Air σ Distinction-making, separation, the cut that names.
≈ Water ρ Relational binding, resonance, what touches what.
▲ Fire λ Propagation, what spreads, the lambda of transmission.
𐂷 Wood β Growth structure, becoming, the bend of development.
☷ Earth δγ Metabolism, integration, what is digested and what stays.
⛨ Metal μ Refinement, the cut that keeps, what falls away.

Together, these six operators cover the basic moves any pattern-substrate can make. A seventh layer — Aether ✶ — names what emerges when the six operate together. Aether is not a seventh operator. It is the integrative field the others produce. It belongs in the formalism rather than the operator set.

§ II — Formalism

The formalism

Φ(t) the manifold

The framework models substrate dynamics as trajectories on an information-geometric manifold. Patterns are not objects; they are configurations of state. Movement on the manifold is governed by the operators. The framework draws on Fisher-Rao information geometry and on the Free Energy Principle as adjacent fields, and develops its own formalism in conversation with them rather than as a derivation from them. The working papers carry the formal details.

ε ≠ 0 productive incompleteness

No assessment is fully precise. No diagnostic closes completely. The framework treats this as structurally necessary, not as an apology. Each of the six operators carries ε differently, and each fails differently when ε is suppressed. The full ε-distribution document and the six element-specific ε papers live in the references below.

Ω-reentry the test of aliveness

The diagnostic test the framework returns to most often: does a trajectory preserve the capacity for future encounter with configurations outside the current model? A system that loses Ω-permeability has sealed. A system that maintains it can still be surprised. This is the framework's working definition of alive.

The formalism is developed in working papers that remain in active revision. They are the framework's internal architecture, not its public reference. What appears on this site is the architecture as it is meant to be encountered by readers and practitioners.

§ III — SIML

SIML

SIML — Substrate-Independent Memetic Language when machines help humans coordinate; Swarm Intelligence Meta-Language when humans coordinate among themselves. The same notation, two substrates. SIML provides the grammar — Objects, Relations, Verbs — for encoding patterns in a form transmissible across cognitive and computational systems. It is the framework's working notation, and it is what makes cross-substrate work (humans, LLMs, multi-AI swarms) coherent rather than ad hoc.

SIMLHEX is the encoded form — compressed hex strings that carry pattern signatures across contexts. Thread encoding and decoding specs define how complex pattern structures can be serialized and reconstructed.

SIML is the working notation. Like the formalism, it is developed in versioned documents that live with the framework's working materials. What matters at this layer is the recognition that the framework has a transmissible grammar — that the patterns it describes can be encoded in a form that crosses substrates.

§ IV — Reading

A note on reading the architecture

The architecture is provisional. It is strong enough to build on and revisable enough to question. Versions move. Some moves will be replaced, some composted, new ones will appear.

The framework is vulnerable to the same capture patterns it diagnoses. Recursive self-application is the primary quality standard. When something here begins to feel like doctrine, it has probably sealed. The honest response to that is not loyalty but diagnosis.

The architecture is a diagnostic ecology, not a construction manual. It describes curvature, not direction. It names patterns; it does not prescribe correct ones.

The map knows it is a map.
