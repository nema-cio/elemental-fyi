Patterns are alive. They move through us, shape what we notice, and decide what we pass on.

Ideas, beliefs, moods, ways of seeing — they behave like living things. They find hosts. They compete for room. They borrow our voices and our attention to make more of themselves.

Some of them help us breathe. Some quietly close us in. The trouble is they rarely announce which is which. By the time we notice the shape of one, we are usually already inside it, already speaking from inside it, already passing it on.

Think of the difference between a conversation that opens you up and one that subtly traps you — even when both feel agreeable in the moment. Both can be warm. Both can be polite. Only one of them leaves you with more room than you came in with.

We live inside these patterns whether we name them or not. Naming them is not control. It is just the difference between weather you notice and weather you don't.

This is a way of looking. A set of instruments for noticing what is moving through you, and what is moving you.

It is not a belief system. It is not a path.

The map knows it is a map.
