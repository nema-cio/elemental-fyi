Accounts

the elements tell of the humans they have read

Before you hand the elements anything of your own, watch them work. In each account, six of them take up one person's turning — a decision, a season, a stuck place — and read it in turn, each carrying it as far as its element can before handing it to the next.

You will see a single line — a nemetic.φ — crystallize under each element, and the six of them assemble into a chain:

σ∘ρ∘λ∘β∘δγ∘μ

That chain is the whole method, learned by watching it applied to one life instead of explained. When you've seen a reading move, making your own is no longer a leap in the dark.

The humans in these first accounts are composed figures — built from the pattern of many real turnings, never one person's. The way of reading them is real. In time, with consent, the accounts will be of real people who chose to share their readings.
